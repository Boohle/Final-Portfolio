ABOUT ME

I am a young, hardworking female born, bred and buttered in Soweto, Johannesburg born to two blind parents. Having been raised in Johannesburg and under such circumstances, I am an open minded, motivated and action-orientated person who enjoys working with and for people who, like me, want to see a better and more loving world.

It is easy to work with me as I am fast thinking and come up with quick and effective solutions to most problems that arise. Working under pressure and with big workloads comes easily to me as I was taught never to give up. I am the type of person who loves learning new things and therefore enjoys challenges. I make it my goal to finish and understand anything and everything I have started, making it easier to work with me as I do not have to be told the same thing twice.

“Ngeke ngihlulwe yinto engakhulumi.” -My Dad

I am an ADA graduate!!

In all honesty I did not know much about web development before being selected to join the African Developers Academy, as one of twelve women in a 12 month Front-End development programme. I can now confidently say that I have learned a bit more, and the more you know the more there is to know - that is why I have developed a passion for learning. I have learned about HTML, CSS and Javascript which has led me to start thinking broader than I could think before.

Coding has become part of my everyday thinking. I look forward to incorporating it in my everyday life. I believe there are ways to make less fortunate people’s lives better, using technology. With all the qualifications I am passionate about I firmly believe that coding can make a huge difference within the fire and life safety industry. Having studied and worked in the Johannesburg fire department, I believe that coding could help this department move into the fourth industrial revolution with more ease. So to help prevent the large number of current fatalities happening due to a lack of knowledge on what to do in case of an emergency.
