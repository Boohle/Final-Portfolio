My coding journey

It all started with an invite to Jozi Hub on 44 stanley Avenue for a games day on a Saturday morning. There were about 100 ladies invited to get the same opportunity that I knew nothing about, however on that day I finished top of the group and came out as one of the top candidates. I was told I showed particular aptitude in creativity and excelled at the coding requirements of Turtle Academy. When this day ended a selection process started, leaving 30 of us to come in for a 3 day bootcamp. At the end of the 3 days the numbers were to be cut down to 12 candidates, who would have to wait to be contacted by email to see if they were selected.

On 31/07/2019 I was one of the 12 ladies to receive an acceptance email from the academy. Life as I had known it changed after reading it. Classes commenced on the 5/08/2019 and I would be lying if I said it was easy, as it wasnâ€™t. At first I thought coding was impossible but not even a month into it and I had fallen in love. Learning HTML, CSS and Javascript has not been easy however as a person who enjoys learning and overcoming challenges I have certainly enjoyed this process and look forward to furthering my studies in System Development if given the opportunity.

Due to the onset of Covid-19 and the nationwide lockdown, the internships that ADA had initially organised for us, were postponed. I am looking forward to the opportunity to practice my newly acquired skills. My plan is to continue practising as well as to self study using Canva, Codecademy, Free Code Camp, GitHub and any other resources available. As I better my own knowledge I plan to join community outreach programmes that help children learn more about coding. I will be passing as much knowledge as I can while improving my own coding abilities, especially for less fortunate children.
